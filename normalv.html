All About Normal Values

Vital Signs Taking

Vital signs are measurements of the body's most basic functions. Vital signs help detect or monitor medical problems.They can be measured in a medical setting, at home, at the site of a medical emergency, or elsewhere.

Height and Weight of Pediatric Patients

These measurements provide essential data to assess a child's overall health, nutritional status, and physical development. Regular tracking helps identify potential health issues.

Blood Normal Values

Knowing the normal values of blood is crucial for diagnosing and monitoring health conditions. These values serve as benchmarks for determining what is healthy and what indicates potential medical issues.

Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS)

The Glasgow Coma Scale is a tool that healthcare providers use to measure decreases in consciousness. The scores from each section of the scale are useful for describing disruptions in nervous system function and also help providers track changes. It’s the most widely used tool for measuring comas and decreases in consciousness.

Arterial Blood Gas (ABG)

Knowing the normal values of arterial blood gas (ABG) is crucial in medical practice as it provides essential information about a patient's respiratory and metabolic status. ABG analysis measures parameters such as pH, partial pressure of carbon dioxide (PaCO2), and partial pressure of oxygen (PaO2), which help assess the body's acid-base balance and oxygenation.

Electrolytes

Knowing the normal values of electrolytes is crucial in medical practice as they play a vital role in maintaining bodily functions. Electrolytes such as sodium, potassium, calcium, and magnesium are essential for regulating nerve and muscle function, hydration, blood pH, and repairing damaged tissues. Abnormal levels can indicate underlying health issues like dehydration, kidney disease, or heart problems.

Renal Function Tests

Kidney function tests are urine or blood tests that evaluate how well your kidneys are working. Most of these tests measure glomerular filtration rate (GFR). GFR assesses how efficiently your kidneys clear waste from your system.

Liver Function Tests

Liver function tests are blood tests that measure different substances produced by your liver, including proteins, enzymes and bilirubin. High or low levels of different substances can indicate different diseases

Lipid Panel Test

A lipid panel is a common blood test that healthcare providers use to monitor and screen for your risk of cardiovascular disease. The panel includes three measurements of your cholesterol levels and a measurement of your triglycerides.

Pancreatic Enzymes

Pancreas tests help identify problems with your pancreas and diagnose conditions such as pancreatitis and cancer. Your provider will often start with a simple blood test. Pancreas function tests measure your pancreatic enzyme values. Imaging tests can help show inflammation or abnormalities affecting your pancreas.

HbA1c

The hemoglobin A1c test tells you your average level of blood sugar over the past 2-3 months. It's also called HbA1c, the glycated hemoglobin test, or glycohemoglobin.

Total Protein, Albumin-Globulin (A/G) Ratio Test

A total protein test measures the sum of all types of proteins in the blood. Proteins are fundamental to the functioning of the body.
